Key Takeaways

  • Workers in less affluent countries often work significantly longer hours compared to those in wealthier nations.
  • This highlights important insights about economic progress over the past century and underscores the ongoing disparities between countries today.
  • Residents in countries like Myanmar and Cambodia face dual challenges: financial poverty and leisure poverty, driven by low productivity economies that necessitate longer working hours.
  • This lack of spare time often limits opportunities for self-improvement, such as pursuing education or skill development.
  • The data reveals that the disparity in prosperity is not due to differences in work ethic, but rather differences in circumstances and opportunities.
