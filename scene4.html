Technological Innovation Drives Productivity Growth and Boosts National Incomes

  • Technological Innovation— broadly encompassing physical machinery, ideas, knowledge, and processes — enables workers to become significantly more productive.
  • This enhanced productivity leads to higher incomes and eventually reduces working hours.
  • A clear example of technology's impact on productivity is in agriculture, where advancements in machinery, crop varieties, fertilizers, and land management have greatly increased farmers' output.
  • For instance, in the US, farm production per labor hour grew nearly 16-fold between 1948 and 2011. This productivity growth has allowed the US to sustain a growing population while fewer people work in agriculture.
  • Additionally, evidence suggests that shorter working hours can help maintain higher productivity, creating a self-reinforcing link between productivity and working hours.
